Rules of Tic Tac x2

Tic Tac x2 (Ultimate Tic Tac Toe) is a deeply strategic game where small moves dictate major choices.

1. The Grid within a Grid

The board consists of a large 3x3 grid. Inside each of the 9 large cells sits a mini 3x3 tic-tac-toe game. Your goal is to win 3 mini-games in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal row to win the entire match.

2. The Golden Rule of Guidance

You cannot choose which mini-game to play in at will! Wherever your opponent places their mark in their mini-game, they send you to that corresponding position on the large board.

Example: If Player X places a mark in the top-right spot of their current mini-board, Player O's next move MUST be placed inside the top-right mini-board on the large grid.

3. Wildcards

If you are sent to a mini-board that has already been won or is completely full, you get a Wildcard Move! This allows you to place your mark in any available space on any active mini-board.

4. Mini-Board Tie

If a mini-board is full and neither player wins it, it is declared a Draw. It cannot be won by either player, but still counts towards full-board targets (though it won't help you get three in a row).
